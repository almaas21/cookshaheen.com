🍃 Creamy Methi Matar Malai (Dhaba & Restaurant Style)

If you think Methi Matar Malai only tastes good at restaurants and dhabas, then this video will change your mind forever! This recipe is so creamy, so smooth, and so perfectly balanced that even dhaba owners will ask you for the recipe. Watch till the end - if you skip, you'll miss a legendary vegetable dish!

This bitterness-free Methi Matar Malai is ready in just 15-20 minutes and is both restaurant and dhaba style. The secret tips shared in this recipe will make your methi mild, tasty, and completely free of bitterness.

Cuisine North Indian
Prep Time 15 mins
Cook Time 20 mins
Servings 4-5

🥘 Ingredients

For Methi Preparation:

  • 200-250 grams Fresh Methi (Fenugreek) - remove hard stems, use only soft leaves
  • Salt (for removing bitterness)
  • Water (for washing)

For the Gravy:

  • 1 cup Fresh or Frozen Peas
  • 4 Medium Onions (roughly chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Garlic (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger (finely chopped)
  • 2-3 Green Chilies (finely chopped)

For Tempering (Tadka):

  • 2 tablespoons Oil
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
  • Half teaspoon Black Peppercorns
  • 2-3 Cloves (Laung)
  • 1 small piece Cinnamon (Dalchini)

For Cooking Methi:

  • 30-35 grams Butter
  • Little oil (to prevent butter from burning)
  • 30 grams Cashews

Spices:

  • 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric (less than usual for this dish)
  • 1/4 teaspoon Red Chili Powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon Coriander Powder
  • Little Garam Masala
  • 1/4 teaspoon Sugar (balances bitterness and enhances taste)
  • Salt (to taste)

For Finishing:

  • 3-4 tablespoons Fresh Cream (Malai) or homemade cream
  • 1 teaspoon Butter (for rich look)

For Garnish:

  • Fresh Cream
  • Ragaan (Kashmiri red chili fried in oil)
  • Fresh Green Coriander
  • Slit Green Chilies (optional, for spicy lovers)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  • 1
    Prepare the Methi: Remove all hard stems and use only soft green leaves. Chop finely. Massage with salt for 2-3 minutes, then wash thoroughly with 2-3 cups of water. This trick removes all bitterness!
  • 2
    Onion-Cashew Paste: Heat 2 tbsp oil. Sauté cumin, black pepper, cloves, and cinnamon. Add roughly chopped onions and sauté for 2 mins. Add cashews and sauté for 1 min. Add chopped green chilies, a pinch of salt, and 1/4 tsp turmeric. Cool and blend into a smooth paste.
  • 3
    Prepare Peas: Boil fresh peas for 2 minutes and give an ice water shock to retain their bright green color.
  • 4
    Cook the Methi: In a pan, melt 30-35g butter with a little oil. Sauté chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Add the chopped methi and sauté on low flame for 2-3 minutes.
  • 5
    Add Spices and Gravy: Add 1/4 tsp red chili powder, 1/4 tsp coriander powder, and a pinch of garam masala. Mix well. Add the onion-cashew paste. Simmer until oil separates. Add 1/4 tsp sugar to balance bitterness.
  • 6
    Finish: Stir in 3-4 tbsp fresh cream. Mix until evenly combined. Add 1 tsp butter for extra richness. Turn off the gas.
  • 7
    Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh cream, ragaan (Kashmiri chili oil), and fresh coriander. Serve hot with naan or jeera rice.

💡 Chef's Secret Tips

  • Remove Bitterness: Massaging with salt and washing is the key secret!
  • Ice Water Shock: Retains the bright green color of the peas.
  • Cashew Magic: Provides the silky, restaurant-style texture.
  • Sugar Balance: Just a pinch enhances the overall flavor profile.

📝 Notes

  • Use a thick-bottomed pan to prevent burning.
  • You can use garlic-ginger paste instead of fresh chopped.
  • Store-bought cream or homemade cream both work well.
  • This dish is best served immediately while hot.
